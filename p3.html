1. SETUP AND DATA LOADING Open Power BI Desktop. Click 'Get Data' > 'Excel workbook'. Select the 'Superstore Sample Dataset' and load the 'Orders' table. Confirm fields like Order Date, Sales, Profit, and Category appear in the Data pane. 2. KPI CARDS (TOP OVERVIEW) Click the 'Card' icon in the Visualizations pane. Drag 'Sales' into the Fields well. (Result: 2.30M) . Create a second Card and drag 'Profit' into Fields. (Result: 286.40K) . Create a third Card and drag 'Quantity' into Fields. (Result: 38K) . Place these at the top of your dashboard for a quick overview. 3. PROFIT BY SUB-CATEGORY (BAR CHART) Click the 'Clustered Bar Chart' icon. Drag 'Sub-Category' to the 'Y-axis' (or Axis field). Drag 'Profit' to the 'X-axis' (or Values field). Purpose: To compare profit performance across different product sub-categories. 4. PROFIT BY YEAR AND QUARTER (LINE CHART) Click the 'Line Chart' icon. Drag 'Order Date' to the 'X-axis'. In the X-axis field, right-click 'Order Date' and ensure it is set to 'Date Hierarchy'. Remove 'Month' and 'Day' so only 'Year' and 'Quarter' remain. Drag 'Profit' to the 'Y-axis'. Observation: Higher profits in 2019 indicate peak business periods. 5. REGIONAL AND SEGMENT ANALYSIS (DONUT CHARTS) Profit by Region: Select 'Donut Chart'. Drag 'Region' to Legend and 'Profit' to Values. Note: West Region should be highest (37.84%). Profit by Segment: Select 'Donut Chart'. Drag 'Segment' to Legend and 'Profit' to Values. Note: Consumer Segment should be highest (46.83%). Profit by Category: Select 'Donut Chart'. Drag 'Category' to Legend and 'Profit' to Values. Note: Technology should be highest (50.79%). 6. THE MASTER MATRIX (DETAILED TABLE) Click the 'Matrix' icon. Rows: Drag 'Sub-Category'. Columns: Drag 'Region' (Central, East, South, West). Values: Drag 'Profit'. This creates the detailed breakdown showing totals for items like Accessories (41,936.64) and Phones (44,515.73). OPTIONAL... Step-by-Step: Adding the State Slicer Select the Slicer Tool: In the Visualizations pane, click the Slicer icon (it looks like a funnel inside a small square). Assign the Field: Drag the State field from your Data pane into the Field well of the Slicer. Change to Checklist Style: By default, it might appear as a list. To ensure it has checkboxes like the image, click the Format visual (paintbrush) icon. Go to Slicer settings > Options > Style. Select Vertical list. Enable "Select All" (Optional): In the same Slicer settings menu, go to Selection. Toggle Show "Select all" to On. This allows you to reset the filter quickly. Positioning: Resize the slicer into a tall, thin rectangle and move it to the left side of your canvas to match the "Sample Super Profit Report" layout.
